Bucharest, Romania- September 2023



Touristic Attractions

We arrived at the Bucharest North train station on Tuesday morning.

Since we had 5 hours until our check-in, we decided to walk to the motel, while checking off of our list a few touristic attractions.

We passed by Herastrau Park, the famous House of Parliment and took some pictures, and, of course, we could not pass it without taking a look at the great People's Salvation Cathedral.

Our motel was near the center of Bucharest and I absolutely recommend you stay at a place that is as near as possible to the city center.

The beauty of Bucharest is best seen at night, so you should not waste a beautiful evening in Bucharest by walking in a periferic area.

We walked through the center all day, and finally, we remained at a beautiful cafe on the side of river "Dambovita".

The next day, we visited other tourist attractions such as The Little Paris Museum, Stavropoleos Monastery and the Revolution Square (the place where Nicolae Ceausescu had his last speech).

We walked on the Victory Avenue and we has a relaxing moment in Cismigiu Park.

After we took a delightful walk in the Bothanical Garden, we stopped for a great pizza at Pizza Hut.

On the last day, after the check-out, we walked again to the train station and stopped at Cotroceni Museum, which I really recommend visiting.

It is a beautiful place, full of history and affordable.

Traditions & Food

Romanian food is absolutely delicious. It combines cultures and traditions of all the ancestors of this beautiful place.

Our favourite meal was "sarmale", which are pickled cabbage rolls filled with meat and rice, served with fresh bread and sour cream.

If you arrive in this balcanic area, you should definitely try "shaorma", which is a pita roll filled with all kinds of goodies.

And finally, our favourite dessert was "papanasi", which are the best doghnuts served with sour cream and homemade jam.
